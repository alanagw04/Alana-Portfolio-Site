About Me

Alana Wilkes PVAMU Student
"Security systems fail when they ignore the human elements of trust, friction, and capability."

I study at the intersection of technology and human behavior. As a Computer Science student with a Psychology minor at Prairie View A&M University, I examine how security parameters can be configured to protect populations that are often left behind by standard industry practices.

My career goals center on cyberlaw, digital compliance, and privacy policy. I intend to pursue a Master's degree in Cybersecurity Law and Procedure at Texas A&M to analyze how legal frameworks govern digital access, consumer protection, and cybersecurity compliance.

Too often, digital safety standards assume perfect motor skills, high cognitive bandwidth, and endless memory. When design choices lock out older adults, individuals with cognitive differences, or busy caregivers, it is a failure of both accessibility and security. I seek to build tools that enforce solid security guidelines without compromising human dignity or usability.

Selected Cases

The Problem: Authentication systems operate on high-friction assumptions—expecting users to read micro-fonts, recall intricate secrets, parse obscure symbols, and coordinate multi-device tasks. This introduces severe barriers for older adults, caregivers, and users with cognitive or physical disabilities.

How it works: "Secure but Simple" is a client-side prototype that screens users through an accessible Needs Assessment quiz, scores six distinct authentication modes, adjusts outcomes via a custom priority slider, generates a step-by-step security action plan, and offers interactive CAPTCHA and scam-checking tools.

The Project: A Python utility designed to parse terms of service and privacy agreements. It highlights potentially non-compliant data collection clauses using structural heuristics and keywords aligned with GDPR, CCPA, and COPPA frameworks.

By analyzing text density, passive voice constructs, and vague verbs ("may share", "third-party partners"), the script evaluates policies for cognitive readability score (Flesch-Kincaid) alongside legal clarity.

The Project: A psychological usability assessment investigating the impact of temporal limits and structural complexity on user anxiety during multi-factor authentication (MFA) setup tasks.

The study gathered response latency, error frequency, and self-reported anxiety indexes (using Likert-scale surveys) from distinct age brackets. Results indicated that strict time-based verification increases login failure rates by 42% for older adults.
